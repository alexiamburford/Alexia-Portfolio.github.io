About Me

I am a scholar at the illustrious Howard University, majoring in Psychology with a minor in Afro-American Studies. Now thriving in my junior year, I am a proud member of the College of Arts and Sciences Honors Program, Cohort 1 of the Humanities and Social Sciences Scholars Program, and the Flagship E-Fellows Program. I am deeply committed to research and plan to pursue a Ph.D. in Psychology after completing my undergraduate studies. My research interests focus on how environmental factors—particularly school environments—shape the self-perception and decision-making of African American girls. In pursuit of my doctoral goals, I have conducted research at both Brown University and The Johns Hopkins University, and I have presented my work at the Leadership Alliance National Symposium (LANS) and the Annual Biomedical Research Conference for Minoritized Scientists (ABRCMS). I also serve as a research student in the Belonging, Learning, Identity, and Culture (BLIC) Lab at Howard University under the mentorship of Dr. Davis Dixon. Beyond my academic pursuits, I am actively engaged in student leadership across campus. I serve within the 65th Administration of the Howard University Student Association as the Large Scale Projects Sub-Lead, hold the position of Vice President in Precious Pearls Youth Outreach, and act as the Membership Outreach Chair for the National Society of Leadership & Success—among numerous other involvements that reflect my commitment to service, empowerment, and community.
